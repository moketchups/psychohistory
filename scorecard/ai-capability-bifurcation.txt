- **Anthropic Mythos:** Anthropic halted broader release of its newest model, citing "high-severity vulnerability" finding capability against major operating systems. (Business Insider, Apr 2026)
- **Anthropic Glasswing:** "Anthropic's most powerful AI cyber model is too dangerous to" release. A separately gated capability — not the same as Mythos. (VentureBeat)
- **DOD ultimatum:** "In late February, the U.S. Department of Defense issued Anthropic a formal ultimatum: grant the military unrestricted [access]." State actor demanding access to the gated tier. (Fazal Sec roundup, citing Anthropic disclosures)
- **Unreleased model leak:** Anthropic accidentally left details of an unreleased model in an unsecured data trove. Confirms the existence of models that exist but are not deployed. (Fortune, Mar 26 2026)
- **DeepSeek withholding:** DeepSeek withholding its latest model from US chipmakers (Nvidia, AMD) — Chinese version of the same pattern from the other side of the bifurcation. (Yahoo Finance)
- **US withdraws from Global AI Safety Report:** State-level meta-move — declining the multilateral disclosure regime entirely. (TIME)
- **Anthropic RSP v3.0 + ASL-3 Security Standard:** Internal capability tier publicly named, with deployment gating logic written into policy. The bifurcation is not a leak — it's now codified company process.

**Structural significance:** This is the same two-tier pattern the engine tracks at the compute layer (Genesis substrate vs civilian grid), the information layer (Dead Internet vs curated channels), and the financial layer (Aladdin vs retail markets) — now visible at the AI capability layer. Public demos vs gated frontier. The DOD ultimatum is the load-bearing detail: it transforms the story from "company safety policy" into "state-corporate negotiation over selective capability access." Same architecture as the SCIF model — capability exists in classified space, civilian deployment is downstream, military gets a backchannel.

**Watch for:** Named models entering "indefinite hold" status that previously had release dates (Mythos, Glasswing established the precedent — track which others follow). Government access agreements being struck in lieu of release (NDAs, Project Stargate-tier deals). RSP / responsible scaling policy version bumps that move capability thresholds upward. EU AI Act enforcement actions or carve-outs for "national security." Any leak/disclosure showing what's actually in the gated tier. DeepSeek-style adversary mirroring: Chinese, Russian, Israeli labs adopting the same withholding posture.

**Falsification:** If withheld models surface as deployed in commercial products within 6 months of being declared "too dangerous," the gating is PR theater rather than real capability separation. If RSP versions never increase capability thresholds, the policy is performative not load-bearing.
