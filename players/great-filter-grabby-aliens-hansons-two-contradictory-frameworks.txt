4:T6f9,**Plain read:** The same economist published two cosmological models that say opposite things. One explains why we see nothing (a filter wipes civilizations out). The other predicts we should see expanding civilizations soon (or we are puzzlingly early). The contradiction IS the analytical scaffold. **Great Filter (Hanson 1996, 1998 with Sandberg + Bostrom):** absence of visible civilizations means one or more steps in the chain are extremely improbable. Past-filter candidates: abiogenesis, prokaryote→eukaryote transition (~2 billion years on Earth), sexual reproduction, Cambrian explosion conditions, mammalian niche post-Chicxulub. Future-filter candidates: AGI, synthetic biology, nuclear proliferation. **If filter is past:** Rare Earth scenario; we are alone in the observable universe. **If filter is future:** civilizations consistently extinct themselves at thresholds we are currently crossing. **Grabby Aliens (Hanson 2021, arXiv 2102.01522):** loud civilizations expand visibly at fractional-c, permanently transforming their region. Three model parameters: expansion speed, birth power (hard evolutionary steps), origin date. Empirical prediction: humanity is arriving puzzlingly early; deadline-setting encounter expected on cosmological timescales. **Engine framing:** Great Filter mathematics share 'civilizational-failure' grammar with the engine's structural-pressure windows (2032 magnitude 2.078, 2027 1.871, 2030 1.802, May 2040 Phoenix transit), but the mapping is DESCRIPTIVE ANALOGY, not engine-prediction-merger.
