Allen Dulles (1893-1969)

CIA Director — Nazi Banking Bridge

First civilian CIA Director (1953-1961). Sat on the board of Schroeder, Rockefeller and Company, which handled Hitler's personal funds. His law firm Sullivan & Cromwell represented Nazi-linked companies. Brother John Foster Dulles (Secretary of State) completed the pair: one controlled intelligence, the other diplomacy. Together they ensured continuity between the pre-war banking networks that funded Hitler and the post-war intelligence apparatus that absorbed Nazi scientists (Operation Paperclip). The Bank for International Settlements (Basel, 1930) laundered Nazi funds — its first president was Gates McGarrah (Federal Reserve officer, grandfather of future CIA director Richard Helms). The intelligence-finance nexus is not a conspiracy theory; it's the organizational chart.
