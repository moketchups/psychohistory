The Khazarian Attribution — Historical + Genetic Reality

2025 Scholarly Consensus: Koestler Hypothesis Collapsed

Historical foundation: Khazar Khaganate (c. 650-965 CE) ruling elite likely adopted Judaism in the 9th century. Documented sources: Hasdai ibn Shaprut correspondence, Kiev Letter (~930s), Al-Masudi 10th-c. account, Ibn Fadlan, Christian of Stavelot.
Modern popularization: Arthur Koestler *The Thirteenth Tribe* (1976) — Koestler himself Jewish; argued with sincere anti-antisemitic motivation to undercut ethnic antisemitism. Subsequent weaponization by antisemitic + anti-Zionist actors reversed Koestler's intent.

2010-2025 genetic consensus (multi-disciplinary): Hammer et al. *PNAS* 2000 — Y-chromosome Middle Eastern continuity.

Archaeological + philological consensus: Medieval Arab + Persian geographers (Ibn Fadlan, al-Istakhri, al-Masudi) documented Khazaria politics + religions extensively but recorded NO mass Jewish population + NO westward migration. Cairo Geniza (hundreds of thousands of medieval Jewish documents 9th-13th c.) contains no Khazar-community records. Khazar urban center excavations yielded no mikvaot + no Hebrew inscriptions + no Jewish material culture.
Stampfer Rhineland hypothesis: Eastern European Jews descend from small Czech + southeastern Germanic migrant group moving east for economic reasons 12th-15th c. Has replaced Khazar hypothesis.
Verdict: the Khazarian-ancestry claim as applied to modern Ashkenazi Jews is empirically void. The 'Khazarian Mafia' modern conspiracy term is a rhetorical dog-whistle; extremism researchers (Institute for Strategic Dialogue) note it functions identically to traditional antisemitic tropes ('Rothschild,' 'Zionist,' 'International Banker') while providing plausible-deniability cover.
