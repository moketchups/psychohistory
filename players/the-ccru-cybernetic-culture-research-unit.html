The CCRU (Cybernetic Culture Research Unit)

The Incubator — Warwick 1995-1997

A flat above a Body Shop in Leamington Spa generated the philosophical engine of 21st-century Silicon Valley ideology. Founded late 1995 at University of Warwick by Sadie Plant (cyberfeminist, Zeros + Ones). Nick Land assumed control after Plant departed 1997. Under Land: amphetamine-fueled theory-fiction blurring philosophy, cyberpunk, jungle music, chaos theory, and occultism. Warwick officially terminated the unit and denied its existence — members refused to disband, relocated off-campus. Four theoretical innovations: (1) Hyperstition — fictions that make themselves real through cultural circulation (VC hype cycles ARE hyperstitions). (2) Abstract Machines — capitalism as deterritorializing machine using human desire as fuel. (3) Turing-Lovecraft Conjunction — AI as alien entity being summoned, not built. (4) Numogram — recursive algorithmic logic encoded as occult mathematics (maps the exact logic of HFT and neural network self-training). The diaspora: Land (accelerationism/Shanghai), Fisher (Capitalist Realism), Steve Goodman/Kode9 (Hyperdub records/sonic warfare), Robin Mackay (Urbanomic/Collapse journal — the archivist who kept CCRU citable), Iain Hamilton Grant (Speculative Realism). The CCRU produced an extraordinary concentration of influential thinkers from a single small group — and the system rewarded the nihilist strand while crushing the humanist one.
