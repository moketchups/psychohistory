Mark Fisher (1968-2017)

The Counter-Thesis — Turchin + Fisher + Phoenix Convergence

Capitalist Realism (2009): "It is easier to imagine the end of the world than the end of capitalism." Same CCRU origin as Land, opposite conclusion. Where Land saw the autonomous engine and commanded acceleration, Fisher diagnosed that the system survives not by repression but by eliminating the capacity to imagine alternatives. Generates reflexive impotence — subjects know the system is broken, understand the trajectory is terminal, yet feel powerless to alter it. k-punk blog (2003-2017): the most influential left cultural theory blog of its era. Acid Communism (unfinished): attempted to recover the 1960s/70s countercultural moment when alternatives were still imaginable. Fisher died by suicide in 2017 — a casualty of the very depressive anhedonia he diagnosed. The asymmetry of systemic reward: Fisher identified the trap and tried to dismantle it — destroyed. Land embraced the trap and provided philosophical justification for human obsolescence — elevated to holy writ by $42B VC firms. The system algorithmically rewards those who provide intellectual cover for its expansion while crushing those who attempt to model its exit. BST alignment (Tier 4): Fisher's Capitalist Realism maps with exact mathematical precision to BST. No bounded system can model its own source → no bounded system can model its own alternative. Land assumes infinite acceleration toward transcendence; Fisher recognized the boundary. Fisher was right. The engine credits him.
