Historical Limit — Complexity Collapse

"The Collapse of Complex Societies" (1988). Each marginal unit of complexity costs more energy to maintain than the previous. Diminishing returns on complexity are universal across civilizations. When maintenance costs exceed available energy, collapse is not failure — it is an economizing process. Rome, Maya, Chaco Canyon: same pattern. BST integration: directly maps to the Scaling Illusion and Landauer's Wall. The Genesis Mission's exponentially growing compute requirements will hit Tainter's ceiling. You cannot scale complexity without exporting entropy, and there is nowhere left to export it.
