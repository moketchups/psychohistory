The Gladio Exposure Chain (1990-1992)

How Disclosure Was Forced, Not Volunteered

Judge Felice Casson (Venice): investigating the 1972 Peteano terrorist attack, traced explosives to Gladio arms caches. January 1990: began formal investigation of Gladio archives. July 1990: PM Giulio Andreotti allowed Casson access to Palazzo Braschi (SISMI headquarters) where Gladio documents were stored. August 2, 1990: Andreotti first confirmed Gladio's existence to parliament. October 23-24, 1990: last ACC meeting in Brussels. October-November 1990: Andreotti made fuller public speeches. President Francesco Cossiga attempted to suppress the investigation, threatened to close it, and ultimately stepped down April 1992. This timeline matters: disclosure was forced by a judge's investigation, not volunteered by the state. It took 42 years (1948-1990) to surface. The Genesis Mission has been running for less than 1 year.
