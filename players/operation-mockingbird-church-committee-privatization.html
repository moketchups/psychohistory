Phase I — The State-Media Apparatus (1948-1976)

Frank Wisner's "Mighty Wurlitzer" (OPC, 1948): systematic CIA recruitment of journalists, infiltration of media organizations, clandestine funding of cultural fronts. Carl Bernstein (Rolling Stone, 1977): 400+ American journalists carried out CIA assignments over 25 years. Apex institutions: NYT (Arthur Hays Sulzberger, Cy Sulzberger signed secrecy agreement, published CIA briefings verbatim under own byline), CBS (William Paley), Time Inc (Henry Luce). Joseph Alsop, Stewart Alsop as witting assets. Church Committee (1975-76): 126 meetings, 800 witnesses, 110,000 documents. Confirmed CIA-media nexus. Exposed SHAMROCK, MKULTRA, COINTELPRO, assassination plots. Led to permanent Senate Intelligence Committee + EO 11905. But: Bernstein argued Committee covered up the true extent because full exposure would permanently embarrass the most powerful publishers in American journalism. Second-order effect: forced privatization — direct CIA payroll became corporate consolidation and NGO cutouts. The architecture survived; it shed its skin. Udo Ulfkotte ("Bought Journalists," 2014): documented how CIA and BND continue bribing/coercing European journalists — architecture evolved rather than disappeared.
