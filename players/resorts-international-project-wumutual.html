Resorts International / Project WUMUTUAL

CIA Casino Laundering Network

Meyer Lansky → Havana casino empire (under Batista) → expelled by Castro's 1959 revolution → geographic pivot to Bahamas. Lansky used Alvin Malnik as his financial front to establish Paradise Island gambling. A&P heir Huntington Hartford sold Paradise Island interests to James Crosby's Mary Carter Paint Company, which became Resorts International (1968). Crosby partnered with Wallace Groves (convicted stock swindler, Grand Bahama Port Authority). CIA Project WUMUTUAL: CIA file #473 865 links Groves to Lansky/Trafficante as "advisor or possible officer of Project WUMUTUAL entities" (April 1966–1972); CIA OS file #591 722 on Resorts itself = direct operational interest by CIA Cover and Commercial Staff. Dual-purpose node: capital laundering for Lansky syndicate + off-the-books CIA anti-communist operations in the Caribbean. Trump's July 1987 acquisition: $78.9M for 585,067 Class B shares = 72.3% voting control from the Crosby estate. Installed as chairman. Hostile takeover battle with Merv Griffin: Griffin took parent company ($365M), Trump took the unfinished Taj Mahal directly ($273M). By taking Taj Mahal, Trump inherited the physical infrastructure of the WUMUTUAL/Lansky laundering network. Taj Mahal subsequently fined $10M by FinCEN for "willful and repeated" BSA violations spanning back to 2003 — "leaving the financial system unacceptably exposed to illicit actors." Pattern: Trump didn't build a casino empire — he acquired a pre-existing intelligence-crime laundering infrastructure.
