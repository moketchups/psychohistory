The Doctrine Defined

Not triumphant domination — frantic defense

The Donroe Doctrine is the engine's term for a pattern that appears across every civilization at its inflection point: when the system's managers recognize that growth is no longer possible, they shift from expansion to extraction. The doctrine has three phases: (1) Drill — maximize resource extraction before competitors or collapse make it impossible (Arctic drilling, deep sea mining, rare earth scramble, Greenland mineral push). (2) Hoard — secure supply chains and critical infrastructure for the admin class (Genesis Mission, SMR buildout, strategic reserves, gold accumulation). (3) Fortify — build survival architecture that functions independently of the system it's extracting from (orbital data centers, nuclear-powered AI labs, Antarctica positioning). The key insight: this is not 'triumphant global domination.' It is a frantic defense. The people executing this strategy know the system is failing — their actions are the proof.
