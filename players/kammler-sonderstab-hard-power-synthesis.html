Kammler Sonderstab — Hard-Power Synthesis

Technological Hyper-rationalism + Mass-Sacrifice Engine

SS-Obergruppenführer Hans Kammler: head of Sonderstab Kammler. By April 1945 controlled V-1, V-2, Me-262 jet production, the Reich's most advanced secret-weapons programs. Operated Der Riese complex (Lower Silesia, seven underground sites) and the Wenceslas Mine. Associated with Die Glocke — heavily mythologized in postwar literature but anchored in documented underground-industrial operations.
The synthesis: Kammler's operations fused the Order's metaphysical objectives with hyper-advanced lethal technics. This was not separate from the occult; it was the material manifestation. The program ran on the systematic extermination of hundreds of thousands of slave laborers (Mittelwerk alone: ~20,000 inmate deaths). A mass-sacrifice engine driving technological acceleration.
April-May 1945 disappearance: at least four contradictory accounts of Kammler's suicide or death in combat. His subordinates (Rudolph, Debus) and thousands of documents were captured or voluntarily surrendered. The engine reads this ambiguity as the primary node-level operational-continuity divergence; whichever single answer turns out to be true, the subordinates and documents are demonstrably preserved in postwar US infrastructure.
