The Water-Power Nexus

Hydrological Redlining and the Great Lakes Skeleton Key

Great Lakes Compact (2008) should protect 20% of the world's surface freshwater. Foxconn obtained 7M gallons/day exception (2018) for manufacturing that never materialized. Environmental groups (Midwest Environmental Advocates) sued — administrative law judge sided with DNR. Water rights seamlessly transferred to Microsoft when Foxconn retreated. Phase 1 Microsoft: 8.4M gallons/year. City of Racine refused to disclose water consumption data; forced by court order (2025). Power: 3.9 GW from Microsoft + Vantage data centers = exceeds all Wisconsin residential homes combined, more than 3x Point Beach nuclear plant output. Mayor Cory Mason: utility agreements constitute "hydrological redlining." Diverse, lower-income Racine took the financial risk and infrastructure burden; wealthier suburban Mount Pleasant reaps the tax benefits. Modine Manufacturing (Vanguard 9.55%, BlackRock 7.57%) is the only Racine company thriving — manufacturing thermal management systems (cooling) for the very servers that consume the community's water and power. 78% surge in data center sales (late 2025). $180M new orders. Targeting $2B data center revenue by 2028. The structural irony is complete: the only way Racine's economy survives is by servicing the infrastructure that is consuming Racine's resources. The community's livelihood depends on maintaining the architecture of its own extraction. Twin Disc (1918): Vanguard 10.36%, BlackRock 8.84%, 64% institutional ownership. InSinkErator (1927): sold to Whirlpool for $3B (2022) via Emerson. Walker Manufacturing (1888): absorbed by Tenneco. The industrial base that built Racine is now owned by the algorithmic landlords or has been absorbed into conglomerate margin optimization. The water-power nexus is the physical proof: you cannot run the Technate without water. Racine proves the mechanism. The Great Lakes Compact exception obtained under the pretext of human manufacturing was the skeleton key — once the legal precedent exists, the water flows to whatever the Technate needs, regardless of what was promised to the community.
