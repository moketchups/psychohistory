Players/The Donroe Doctrine

Historical Precedents

Same pattern, different costumes

Every bounded system produces a version of the Donroe Doctrine when it approaches its limits: Rome (3rd-5th century): Diocletian's reforms — freeze social mobility, bind workers to land, stockpile gold, build frontier fortifications. Not an expansion strategy — a managed decline strategy. British Empire (1890s-1940s): Resource extraction accelerated across Africa and India while the domestic economy hollowed. Imperial Preference system (Ottawa Agreements 1932) = hoarding. Military bases maintained globally even as the treasury emptied. Soviet Union (1970s-1991): Resource extraction from satellite states accelerated as the core economy stagnated. Military buildup continued despite economic impossibility. The nomenklatura quietly moved assets offshore. Current cycle: The same pattern is visible in the simultaneous push for Arctic drilling, rare earth acquisition, Genesis Mission infrastructure, and orbital compute — all while the domestic economy shows stress signals (VIX 31, recession warnings, DOGE theater).
