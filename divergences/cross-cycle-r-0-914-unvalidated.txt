In plain language: when the engine overlays one 138-year cycle on another (e.g., 1764-1902 vs 1902-2040), the pattern of events — wars, financial crises, technological disruptions, institutional failures — matches with 88-94% correlation. This is the mathematical basis for the engine's predictions: if the pattern held for 4 previous cycles, the current cycle's trajectory can be projected. <strong>The divergence:</strong> this correlation is internally consistent but externally unvalidated. No peer-reviewed statistical analysis has confirmed the methodology. Potential issues: (1) overfitting — with enough historical events, any 138-year window can be made to correlate with another, (2) selection bias — the anchor dates chosen may unconsciously favor pattern-matching, (3) the correlation methodology itself has not been disclosed in enough detail for independent replication. The r=0.914 is the engine's strongest mathematical claim and simultaneously its most vulnerable. <strong>Falsification:</strong> independent replication by a statistician with no access to the Root Source framework, using only the published anchor dates and the described methodology. If the correlation holds under adversarial analysis, it's the engine's most powerful validation. If it doesn't, the predictive framework needs fundamental revision.
