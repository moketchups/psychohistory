What is Bromley-Powell Policy-Practice vs Means-Ends Decoupling Distinction (2012)?

Plain read: A correction to outdated 1970s Meyer-Rowan organizational-decoupling theory. Patricia Bromley + Walter Powell *From Smoke and Mirrors to Walking the Talk* (2012) demonstrated that in modern audit societies — environments with extreme transparency + digital surveillance + heightened regulatory scrutiny — organizations CANNOT sustain traditional policy-practice decoupling (publicly announce one thing while internally doing another). The exposure-risk is too severe. High pressure forces SUBSTANTIVE COMPLIANCE, coupling announcement to substrate. The decoupling shifts downstream to means-ends decoupling — the substrate practices get implemented exactly as announced but fail to achieve intended systemic outcomes due to field opacity + complex causality. Engine-integrated Apr 25 2026 via the Substrate-vs-Announcement Layer Divergence adjudication, which was promotion-denied to Layer 1 partly because it operated on outdated Meyer-Rowan model. TECHNICAL: Bromley & Powell, 'From Smoke and Mirrors to Walking the Talk: Decoupling in the Contemporary World,' *Academy of Management Annals* 6(1) 2012. Establishes that audit-society institutional-pressure dynamics differ structurally from 1970s-era pressure dynamics. Modern decoupling pattern: substantive compliance at policy-practice level + downstream means-ends failure due to causal opacity. The engine's Substrate-vs-Announcement Layer Divergence framing has been corrected to track means-ends decoupling specifically in domains with high deception-tolerance (statecraft, military, central-banking), NOT as universal pressure-response.
